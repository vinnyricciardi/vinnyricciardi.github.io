About

From the field to production AI — and back again

I started my career doing fieldwork in Southeast Asia and West Africa — biodiversity surveys, value chain assessments, the kind of work where you're in a truck at dawn and presenting to a ministry by afternoon. That led to a PhD, collaborations with NASA and the UN FAO, and a chapter in the World Bank's flagship report on data for policy.

Then I pivoted to industry. Over the past five years I've built production ML and AI systems at scale — agentic AI processing 500K+ daily calls, distributed video pipelines on Kubernetes, RAG systems, voice AI, LLM evaluation infrastructure serving 100M+ users. I've led teams, cut millions in costs, and shipped systems that work.

Now I'm bringing those capabilities back to where they matter most. AI isn't something to fear or pause — it's here, and the organizations working on health, climate, education, and humanitarian response need senior technical guidance to use it well. That's what Glide AI does.

How It Works

Strategic advisory, not outsourced engineering

I work as an embedded strategic advisor — the senior AI perspective your team needs without a full-time executive hire. Engagements are scoped tightly so you get clarity and direction without scope creep.

This isn't implementation work. I'm not writing your production code. I'm telling you what to build, how to architect it, who to hire to build it, and what pitfalls to avoid — based on having built these systems myself at enterprise scale.

Typical engagement

Most advisory relationships run at roughly 10 hours per month — enough for regular working sessions, architecture reviews, hiring support, and async guidance. Engagements are structured around clear deliverables with defined timelines, not open-ended retainers. If you have a specific project that needs scoping, we can structure a shorter engagement around that.
